Foundation

Built by engineers, sustained by alumni.

From a single brainstorming meeting at the Trivandrum Club to a global network spanning twenty chapters — the CETAA story, in three chapters.

1939

The College

CET opens as Travancore's first engineering college, with three branches — Civil, Mechanical and Electrical — and eighteen students in each.

1976

The Association

Dr. R. Ravikumar convenes the first brainstorming meeting; CETAA's bye-laws are passed at the inaugural General Body Meeting that December.

Today

The Network

47,000 members across 20 chapters worldwide, sustaining scholarships, mentorship, and the campus that shaped every one of us.

Giving Back

Three schemes. One purpose — no CETian left behind.

Alumni-funded support for students who need it most, sustained year after year by contributions from across the world.

G
Growing Engineers Monetary Support

GEMS

Annual scholarships for meritorious and needy students — born out of the CETAA Global Meet in Dubai, 2010.

C
Continuous Assistance for Rising Engineers

CARE

Sustained financial assistance through a student's full course of study at CET, year on year.

H
Helping Outstanding Pupils in Education

HOPE Plus

Led by the UAE chapter, supporting school, college and professional students beyond CET alone.
